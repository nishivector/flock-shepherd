ONE VERB: Shepherd
GAME IN 10 WORDS: A lone pulse herds a luminous flock through predator clouds
WHAT THE PLAYER MASTERS: Positioning your node relative to the flock to bias its direction through proximity alone — never touching, only influencing
THE WOW MOMENT: Hold perfectly still for 4 seconds — the flock stops orbiting and compresses into a tight comet spear that punches straight through a barrier you had no other way past
WHY RICARDO: R-Type — reading enemy cloud formations and timing your flock commitment before the window closes. Metal Slug — the threat is always visually legible; a tightening predator spiral vs. your expanding flock is pure spatial threat-reading.
EMOTIONAL ARC:
  start: move and the flock scatters, feels like herding smoke
  groove: you discover stillness attracts, movement repels — the flock orbits you like a slow galaxy you can steer
  climax: threading a full flock through a needle-thin corridor while a double spiral predator cloud tightens on both sides — you have one comet window
WHAT MAKES IT HARD: The flock has 2-second inertia — your repositioning affects where they will be, not where they are; you are always commanding the future, playing 2 beats ahead
SKILL CEILING: 1x player chases the flock from behind, barely guiding; 10x player uses predator cloud walls as compression tools, squeezing the flock into comet form on purpose and releasing it like a slingshot through precision gaps
VISUAL: #4A7FA5 (noon-sky teal background), #F0C842 (gold flock glow), #E05535 (predator cloud ember-red) — distant top-down, Studio Ghibli atmospheric scale — tiny figures, vast sky
AUDIO: 72 BPM, slow heartbeat sub-bass with shimmering textured birdsong above it; signature SFX: a deep resonant whoosh-burst when the flock coheres into tight comet formation
NOT: No tower defense placement; no spatial memorisation maze
GAME NAME HINT: flock-shepherd
